This project examines how game-native AI companions enter livestream interaction. Rather than treating companion utterances only as system outputs, it studies how streamers recognize, ignore, amplify, reinterpret, or repair them in front of an audience.

The project asks how semantically inappropriate, awkward, or transgressive AI utterances can become performatively valuable, and how authorship and accountability are negotiated when generated language becomes part of a streamer's public performance.

Key themes include:

- spontaneous human–AI co-performance;
- streamer repair and improvisation;
- audience uptake and community meaning-making; and
- distributed authorship and responsibility.

**Status:** Ongoing research.
