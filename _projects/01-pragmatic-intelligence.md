Language models often recognize literal content while missing what an utterance is doing socially. This project studies pragmatic inference in indirect, playful, and socially situated Chinese online discourse.

The work develops a context-rich multiple-choice benchmark that reconstructs interactional context rather than presenting isolated comments. It evaluates whether models can infer:

- the target of an utterance;
- the speaker's stance;
- the interactional function of the response;
- the contextual cues supporting an interpretation; and
- the mechanism through which a joke, insinuation, or indirect criticism works.

The broader goal is to develop evaluation methods that treat pragmatic competence as a form of **socially situated reasoning**, rather than as lexical sentiment classification.

**Status:** Ongoing research.
